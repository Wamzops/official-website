Welcome to your new blogging toolkit! This page demonstrates all the elements you can use to make your posts engaging and professional.

## 1. Callout Blocks
Use these for important notes, warnings, or tips. They automatically use your brand colors.

<Info>
**Info Block**: Use this for general information. It uses your primary brand color.
```markdown
<Info color="#ff0000"> Your text here </Info>
```
</Info>

<Alert>
**Alert Block**: Use this for critical warnings or errors.
```markdown
<Alert> Your text here </Alert>
<Alert color=""> Custom Red Alert </Alert>
```
</Alert>

<Warning>
**Warning Block**: Use this for things the user should be careful about.
```markdown
<Warning> Your text here </Warning>
```
</Warning>

<Success>
**Success Block**: Use this for positive outcomes or "done" states.
```markdown
<Success> Your text here </Success>
```
</Success>

## 2. Dynamic Colors & Theme Adaptability

You can now pass custom colors (hex codes or theme tokens) to all callout blocks. To match the "Once UI Look," custom hex codes automatically receive a subtle transparency (10% for background, 30% for border), making them gracefully adapt to both light and dark themes.

### Transparency & Opacity
By default, custom colors are "softened" to bleed through your theme background. You can override this using the `opacity` prop.

- <Info color="#00ffff">**Custom Cyan**: Automatically softened (10% bgColor, 30% borderColor)</Info>
- <Info color="#00ffff" opacity="0.5">**Custom Cyan (Stronger)**: Manual 50% opacity</Info>
- <Info color="accent-alpha-weak">**Native Token**: Uses the built-in system transparency</Info>

### Highlight & Underline
The same logic applies to text formatting.

### Highlights
You can highlight text to make it pop.
- <Highlight>Default Brand Highlight</Highlight> (`<Highlight>text</Highlight>`)
- <Highlight color="yellow">Yellow Highlight</Highlight> (`<Highlight color="yellow">text</Highlight>`)
- <Highlight color="red">Red Highlight</Highlight> (`<Highlight color="red">text</Highlight>`)
- <Highlight color="#6200ee">Custom Hex Highlight</Highlight> (`<Highlight color="#6200ee">text</Highlight>`)

### Underlining
Sometimes you need to emphasize text with an underline.
- <Underline>Standard Primary Underline</Underline> (`<Underline>text</Underline>`)
- <Underline color="red">Red Underline</Underline> (`<Underline color="red">text</Underline>`)

## 5. Editing Colors
To change any of these colors, you can go to `src/resources/custom.css`. Look for the section labeled `Blogging Elements & Custom Formatting`.

- **To change callout colors**: Modify the `Feedback` styles or the variables in `once-ui.config.ts`.
- **To change syntax colors**: Edit the tokens like `.token.keyword` or `.token.string` in `custom.css`.
- **To change list bullets**: Edit the `::before` pseudo-elements in the `.doc-content` section of `custom.css`.
