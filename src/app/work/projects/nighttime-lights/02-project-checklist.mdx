The purpose of completing the following checklist is to provide the rationale
for the project and to validate that the idea makes sense to put Research and
Development (R&D) effort into.

## Research Questions

1. To what extent can nighttime lights explain variation in South Africa’s GDP
   across time and across provinces?
2. Which NTL feature engineering choices (radiance summaries, thresholds,
   spatial smoothing, seasonality controls) are most predictive of GDP?
3. How sensitive are estimates to known NTL confounders (gas flares, ephemeral
   lights, sensor differences, load shedding, aurora, fires)?
4. What is the best strategy to produce robust subnational GDP estimates when
   ground-truth GDP is limited (temporal cross-validation, hierarchical    models, transfer learning)?
5. How do auxiliary covariates (population, built-up area, road density, land
   use, electrification proxies) improve accuracy and interpretability?

#### Expected Key Outputs
>
> - Use VIIRS/DMSP nighttime lights and GIS boundary aggregation to create
>   province-level (and optionally district/municipality-level) features over
>   time.
> - Train baseline models (linear/log-linear, ridge/lasso, random forest/XGBoost;
>   optionally sequence models) to infer GDP.
> - Produce maps and time-series outputs to communicate economic activity
>   patterns and uncertainty.
> - Deliver an extensible workflow that can be adapted to other countries once
>   validated for South Africa.

### The Output

- A GIS and Machine Learning workflow that produces GDP estimates for South Africa at national and
  provincial levels for a selected time range. The timerange will be dependent on what nighttime light data is used; either the DMSP or VIIRS. 
  - A set of maps and charts showing:
    1. Spatial distribution of NTL intensity
    2. NTL-derived feature trends by province

### 2.2 Source of inspiration

> *Explain what inspired this proposal. Provide any relevant details that
> highlight a particular challenge or opportunity this addresses.*

- Nighttime lights have a documented relationship with economic indicators and
  have been used for GDP inference/nowcasting in multiple contexts.
- This approach can complement official statistics with higher-frequency,
  spatially-explicit signals that can be mapped and monitored.
- Example references:
  1. [World Bank Report on Sustainable Cities](https://blogs.worldbank.org/sustainablecities/nighttime-lights-are-revolutionizing-way-we-understand-covid-19-and-our-world)
  

### 2.3 Appropriate task for ML/AI

> *Explain why there is an ML/AI component needed. Provide any information that
> is available to substantiate the appropriateness. Include examples where
> relevant.*

- The mapping from raw raster signals and their covariates to GDP is noisy and
  non-linear. As such, ML can capture interactions and regional heterogeneity.
- Baselines should start simple methods such as log-linear and panel regression, then move to ML
  models once data alignment and leakage checks are correct.
- If extending to [monthly nowcasting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nowcasting_(economics)), sequence models or [state-space](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/state-space-model)
  other approaches may be appropriate.

### 2.4 User Experience

> *Explain who your users are and how they will use the solution/outputs.*

- Researchers and analysts: Reproduce GDP inference experiments and compare
  modeling approaches.
- Policymakers/think tanks: Interpret spatial patterns and monitor changes
  across provinces.
- Data journalists and the public: Consume maps and high-level summaries (with
  clear caveats).

### 2.5 Ethical considerations

> *Explain how the project impacts all people it could affect. Who might be
> harmed by the outputs of this project? Who stands to benefit?*

<Success color="#16a34a">

  - Faster, more spatially-detailed economic insights

<Alert>
Risks:
  - Misinterpretation: NTL is a proxy and can be affected by load shedding,
    sensor artifacts, and sector composition; outputs must include uncertainty
    and caveats.
  - Bias: areas with low electrification or different lighting technology may be

  - Harm from overconfidence: avoid using outputs as sole basis for funding/policy
    decisions without triangulation.

## 3. Reality check

### 3.1 Reasonable expectations

> *Explain the expectations of the output and considerations around the impact
> of mistakes.*

- Expect correlation, not perfect measurement as NTL is informative but incomplete.
- Infer GDP at national/provincial level first; only move
  to finer admin units if labels and validation are adequate.
- Quantify error and communicate uncertainty to avoid overstating precision.

### 3.2 Possible in production

> *Explain what kinds of technology resources are needed to get this project
> into production.*

- Raster processing stack (e.g., Python + rasterio/geopandas/xarray; or Google
  Earth Engine for scalable ETL)
- Storage for rasters and intermediate products
- Compute: moderate CPU for zonal stats; GPU only if deep learning on imagery is
  pursued

### 3.3 Data availability

> *Explain what data is available and how it will be accessed/created.*

- Night lights:
  - Monthly/annual VIIRS products: https://eogdata.mines.edu/products/vnl/
- GDP targets:
  - South Africa GDP data (including provincial GDP for 2008–2019 referenced in
    the existing spreadsheet source)
- Admin boundaries:
  - Statistics South Africa / official boundary datasets (or other authoritative
    boundary sources)
- Optional covariates:
  - Daytime satellite imagery (Sentinel-2 / Landsat) and land cover (Dynamic
    World)
  - Population density layers (e.g., GRID3, Meta HRSL, Kontur)

### 3.4 Data volume

> *Explain the volume of data and time component.*

- NTL rasters can be large, especially at monthly cadence.
- Mitigation: clip to South Africa, use yearly composites for baselines, and
  store derived aggregates as tables.

### 3.5 Required technology & compute

> *Explain what processing power is needed for both R&D and production.*

- R&D: local machine or small cloud instance for yearly composites and
  provincial aggregation.
- Scaling: cloud/GEE for repeated monthly processing or larger-area experiments.

### 3.6 Skills needed

> *List the skills you think will be needed to make the project a success.*

- GIS/Remote Sensing specialist
- Data scientist (panel/time-series modeling)
- Data engineer (ETL, reproducibility)
- Domain reviewer for national accounts / GDP definitions

### 3.7 Ground truth

> *Explain what the ground truth outputs in this project are. Does it exist or
> still need to be created?*

- Primary ground truth: official GDP (national + provincial where available)
  aligned to the same time scale as NTL features.
- A key task is careful alignment of definitions (nominal vs real GDP, base
  year, sector coverage) and revisions.

### 3.8 Logging sanity

> *Explain how confident you are that we can map input to output correctly.*

- Moderate confidence once the pipeline standardizes:
  - consistent boundaries
  - consistent time windows
  - consistent masking for ephemeral lights and artifacts
  - unit conversions and inflation adjustments (if applicable)

### 3.9 Logging quality

> *Explain any data quality issues you foresee.*

- NTL issues: cloud cover artifacts (depending on product), stray light,
  background noise, blooming, sensor changes.
- South Africa-specific confounder to account for: load shedding and
  policy/technology shifts in lighting.

### 3.10 Indifference curves

> *List mistakes that could be in the eventual outputs and indicate how 'bad'
> the errors are relative to one another.*

- Worst: systematic bias that misranks provinces persistently (misleading for
  spatial comparisons).
- Next: overfitting to a short time window (looks great in-sample, fails
  out-of-sample).
- Less severe: noisy year-to-year variation that can be smoothed or constrained.

## 4. Performance metrics

### 4.1 Simulation

> *Explain if the project would benefit from any form of simulation.*

- Optional: synthetic experiments to test robustness to missing lights,
  thresholding choices, and temporal lags.

### 4.2 Metric creation

> *Explain what your performance metric is.*

- Core metrics (depending on target): RMSE/MAE on GDP (often on log-GDP), MAPE
  (with care), and rank-correlation across provinces.
- Use time-based splits (train on earlier years, test on later years) to avoid
  leakage.

### 4.3 Metric review

> *Explain how the performance metric has been reviewed to ensure it can't be
> misinterpreted/abused/gamed.*

- Report multiple metrics and include baselines (e.g., “predict last year’s
  GDP”).
- Always publish uncertainty and residual diagnostics by province.

### 4.4 Metric-loss comparison

> *Explain how the performance metric correlates with a standard loss function.*

- If modeling log-GDP, MSE on log targets aligns with RMSE in log-space and
  stabilizes variance.
- Consider robust losses (Huber) if outliers exist.

### 4.5 Population

> *Explain the statistical population of interest and detail any specific
> exclusions.*

- Focus: South Africa overall and provinces.
- Exclusions/controls: mask gas flares and ephemeral events if present; consider
  excluding saturated pixels depending on product.

## 5. Testing criteria to overcome human biases

### 5.1 Minimum performance

> *Explain minimum acceptance criteria.*

- Must beat a simple baseline (e.g., lag-1 GDP) on a time-based test split.
- Must show stable performance across provinces (no persistent extreme residuals
  without explanation).

## 6. Value creation

### 6.1 Value drivers

> *What benefit is gained by investing effort into this idea?*

- Faster and spatially-explicit economic measurement and monitoring.
- A reusable geospatial methodology that can be extended to other countries and
  other macro indicators.

## Additional Notes

- Decide early whether the main deliverable is (a) provincial GDP inference
  (most defensible given labels) or (b) higher-resolution GDP mapping (harder to
  validate).
- Explicitly document how load shedding and lighting technology changes will be
  treated (covariate, normalization, or robustness checks).
- Keep a clear separation between:
  - ETL (reproducible GIS processing)
  - Modeling (baselines → ML)
  - Communication (maps, uncertainty, caveats)
- Candidate auxiliary datasets for South Africa:
  - Population density: GRID3, Meta HRSL, Kontur
  - Built-up / land use: Dynamic World / GHSL
  - Roads/POIs: OpenStreetMap
